Pediatric Dental Emergencies

Will you know what to do if your child goes through a pediatric dental emergency? Find out how to be a great parent when it comes to dealing with pediatric dental concerns at home:


  • Toothache: First, tell your child to use dental floss to displace any food that may be wedged between the teeth. Second, try gurgling warm water for one minute. If the pain remains, contact Picasso Smiles to explain the problem and schedule an appointment. Never place aspirin or heat on the gum or on the aching tooth. If the face swells, apply a cold compress and inform your Los Angeles dentist ASAP.

  • Knocked Out Permanent Tooth: If you're able to locate the tooth handle it by the crown. Rinse the tooth with water & inspect the tooth for fractures. If it appears to be undamaged, help the patient hold the tooth in place by biting on gauze. If the tooth is damaged, place the tooth in a cup of milk to help maintain the tooth's strength. Timing is critical to save the tooth so contact our office as soon as you can.

  • Knocked Out Baby Tooth: This is extremely common and is not usually an emergency. In most cases, no treatment is necessary. If the tooth is chipped or fractured however, contact your pediatric dentist immediately.

  • Cuts Around Or Inside Mouth: Control swelling by wrapping a cold towel around an icepack. Apply firm but gentle pressure with gauze or a cloth if there is bleeding. If bleeding cannot be controlled by simple pressure, call your doctor or visit the hospital emergency room.

  • Chipped Or Fractured Permanent Tooth: Contact your Los Angeles pediatric dentist immediately. Quick action can save the tooth, prevent infection and reduce the need for extensive dental treatment. Rinse the mouth with water and apply cold compresses to reduce swelling. If possible, locate and save any broken tooth fragments for our dentists to observe.

  • Possible Broken Or Fractured Jaw: Minimize jaw movement and take your child to the nearest hospital emergency room. Talking with a broken or fractured jaw may cause greater injury. Heightened pain should be a clear indicator to keep your mouth closed until you see an expert.

It's always better to be safe than sorry. If there is any question that your may have a pediatric dental emergency, contact your Los Angeles Dentist as soon as possible. We'll be more than happy to comfort you and your child in the case of any pediatric emergencies listed above.
